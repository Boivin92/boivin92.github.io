Kezu, the Crystal Dojo

In a southern canyon, where the sun rarely shines, stands a twisted building of dried wood and purple crystals. A testament of the last century, Kezu the Crystal Dojo stands proud against wind, sand and time.

Long ago, Kezu was the dojo of monks training under a rash master, who sought to govern the local spirits through his martial prowess. As a punishment for his arrogance, elementals stormed the dojo, killed the monks and captured the master. They tortured him for days, feeding him a paste of sand and alchemical fluids. Days turned into weeks, and weeks into months. After too long for him to understand, his mind broke and he fell into madness; it was then that the elementals completed their punishment and started humming together a song from the depths of earth, which turned the blood, flesh and bones of the proud master into crystals of a deep purple color.

Years passed, and the crystal grew quickly, engulfing the dojo as a whole - the master was now a part of his temple. But no matter how much the crystals grow, they can’t seem to get further than a few yards beyond the confines of the building.

Nowaday, people simply stay away from Kezu. A few souls sometimes wander near it, and they say they could hear strange voices from the crystals themselves, as if it beckoned them to step into the building. Some did, but no one came out… except one woman. She was thought dead for a year, but when she returned, her eyes were rough spheres of pink crystals and she wielded a strange martial style.
